Why this project

The smart-meter rollout has shifted utility operations from twice-yearly cumulative reads to half-hourly per-household feeds. Dynamic tariffs, demand-response auctions, and distribution-grid planning all depend on probabilistic, multi-horizon forecasts of household consumption. The challenge is that single-household time series are noisy, behaviour-dependent, and weakly described by demographic proxies, while operational deployments need calibrated quantile outputs across thousands of households.

Energy twin of Project #9 (Favorita retail). Both are hierarchical multi-series forecasting tasks. Retail series are dominated by promotional and calendar shocks; energy series are dominated by weather and intra-day occupancy. The TFT architecture is the canonical multi-horizon probabilistic learner for both.

DACH transferability

  • Stadtwerke and Verteilnetzbetreiber face the same forecasting problem under the Messstellenbetriebsgesetz (MsbG) and BSI Smart Meter Gateway rollout.
  • Section 14a EnWG creates a flexible-load regime parallel to the LCL dToU sub-cohort: heat pumps, wallboxes, battery storage receive incentives for steerable consumption.
  • The architecture, the static-plus-known-plus-observed input partition, the quantile head, and the MinT reconciliation are directly portable.

Methods

Baseline: per-household SARIMA / Prophet

  • Aggregate half-hourly to daily total kWh per household
  • SARIMA(1,1,1)(1,1,1,7) with weather + 3-term yearly Fourier + holiday exogenous
  • Prophet alternative backend with weekly + yearly seasonality and the same regressors
  • Hold out final 60 days; report MAPE / MAE / RMSE at horizons 1d, 7d, 30d (median across households)

Advanced: Temporal Fusion Transformer [Lim 2021]

  • Single TFT trained jointly across households via pytorch-forecasting
  • Static categoricals: LCLid, Acorn_grouped, tariff (Std vs dToU)
  • Known-future reals: weather, day-of-week, day-of-year sin/cos, holiday flag
  • Observed reals: lagged kwh_sum, kwh_mean, kwh_std
  • Hidden 64, attention heads 4, dropout 0.2, lr 3e-4, encoder 60d, decoder 30d
  • Quantile head q10 / q50 / q90; pinball loss

Discussion

  • ACORN as a weak proxy. ACORN segments are a noisy substitute for occupant count, schedule, and appliance portfolio. The LCLid embedding in TFT can subsume ACORN; a planned ablation drops LCLid and keeps Acorn_grouped only.
  • Weather is a forecast. Both models treat weather as known-future. A robustness analysis injecting calibrated weather-forecast noise (e.g. temperature ~ N(forecast, 1.5 degC)) tests deployment overconfidence.
  • dToU as a natural experiment. The dToU sub-cohort was randomised at trial enrolment with day-ahead high-price signals. Future work: counterfactual estimator matched on ACORN, baseline mean kWh, and pre-trial weather window to recover a treatment effect on day-time consumption.
  • Hierarchical reconciliation. MinT [Wickramasuriya 2019] would produce internally coherent forecasts at the household / ACORN / total level, matching the secondary-substation level that DACH Verteilnetzbetreiber operate at.
  • Privacy. Half-hourly per-household readings are potentially re-identifiable; a DACH transfer would need a DSGVO Article 35 Datenschutz-Folgenabschaetzung before any model could train on the German feed.
  • Cost. TFT on 500 households, 30 epochs, batch 64 fits in 4-6 hours on RTX 5090 (24 GB) at FP16. Full 5,567-household sweep is roughly 10x more.

Conclusion

This project implementations a two-tier benchmark on the cleanest large-scale public smart-meter dataset, faithful to the canonical TFT recipe of [Lim 2021] and the LCL deep-learning study of [Fekri 2021]. The methodology, dataset, hierarchy, and metric protocol are pinned now so that the executed run is comparable to published benchmarks. The strategic destination is a transfer-learning step to DACH cohorts under the Smart Meter Gateway and Section 14a EnWG flexible-load regime, not a from-scratch German build.
